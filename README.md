Trading Sentiment Analysis (Fear vs Greed)

Objective

Analyze how trader performance and behavior changes based on market sentiment (Fear vs Greed).

---

Approach

- Compared trader performance metrics like PnL, win rate, and drawdown proxy across sentiment conditions.
- Analyzed behavioral changes such as trade frequency, leverage, and position size.
- Segmented traders into categories for deeper insights.

---

Key Findings

1. Performance vs Sentiment

- Traders tend to perform worse during Fear periods with lower PnL and higher drawdowns.
- During Greed periods, performance improves but risk-taking increases.

2. Behavioral Changes

- Trade frequency and leverage increase during Greed phases.
- Traders become more conservative during Fear phases.
- Long positions dominate in Greed, while short positions increase during Fear.

3. Trader Segments

- High leverage traders face higher losses during volatile (Fear) periods.
- Frequent traders adapt faster to market sentiment.
- Consistent traders maintain stable performance across conditions.

---

Insights

1. High leverage significantly increases downside risk during Fear periods.
2. Overtrading during Greed phases leads to higher volatility in returns.
3. Low-leverage strategies provide more stable outcomes across sentiments.

---

Actionable Strategies

1. Reduce leverage and avoid overtrading during Fear periods.
2. Increase participation during Greed periods with strict risk management.

---

Tools (Assumed)

- Python (Pandas, Matplotlib) or Excel for analysis
